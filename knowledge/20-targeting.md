# Targeting & friendly summons

This file is the canonical reference for telling friendly units apart from
enemies in combat. Cross-reference with HARD RULE #6 in the system prompt.

## Board layout (universal)

In every Slay the Spire 2 combat:

- **LEFT side of the screen**: the player character + any of their summoned
  minions / allies / pets.
- **RIGHT side of the screen**: enemies (1-3 typically, occasionally up to 5
  in elite/boss waves).

The visual cue that *guarantees* a unit is an enemy:

- **An intent indicator above its portrait** — a colored icon showing what
  the enemy will do next turn (red sword for attack with damage number,
  blue/grey shield for block, green up-arrow for buff, purple debuff icon,
  etc.).

Friendly units never have an intent indicator. If a unit on screen has no
intent above it and is on the left side near the player, it is the player's
summon.

## Necrobinder — Osty

The Necrobinder's signature minion is **Osty**, a giant reanimated skeletal
hand. Visual cues:

- Looks like an oversized hand skeleton, often with a blue/cyan flame above
  the wrist.
- Appears NEXT TO the Necrobinder character on the left.
- Has its own HP bar — frequently starts at **1/1** but grows with Summon stacks.
- Has NO intent indicator. (If you see what looks like Osty WITH an intent,
  it's actually a Hand-themed enemy, not Osty — check the side of the
  screen.)

Osty is summoned automatically by the starting relic **Osty's Binding** at
the start of every combat.

Cards that interact with Osty (target Osty, not enemies):

- Bodyguard, Bodyguard+, Reanimate, Spur, Necro Mastery, Friendship,
  Shared Fate — these BUFF/HEAL Osty.
- Unleash, Protector, Fetch, Flatten, Right Hand Hand, Rattle, Sic 'Em —
  these tell Osty to ATTACK an enemy. The card is played from the player's
  hand but the damage source is Osty.

Cards in the Necrobinder pool that are ATTACKS targeting an enemy directly
(not Osty-attacks):

- Scourge / Scourge+, Putrefy / Putrefy+, Sow / Sow+, and most generic
  attack cards. These must target an enemy on the right.

## Regent — token minions

The Regent uses transient token minions and themed cards. Common token /
ally references in combat:

- **Sovereign Blade** — a summoned weapon-blade token that hits enemies on
  its own. Appears next to the Regent on the left, no intent indicator.
- Token attack/skill cards generated by Regent powers (Crash Landing,
  Collision Course, Pillar of Creation interactions) — these are CARDS in
  hand, not units on the board.

If a Regent has a Sovereign Blade or similar token unit on the field, treat
it as a friendly summon by the same rules (left side, no intent).

## Other characters (Ironclad, Silent, Defect, Watcher)

These four legacy characters generally do NOT have permanent summoned units
on the field. Their "minions" come from temporary effects:

- Ironclad — no native summons.
- Silent — no native summons (Wraith Form is a power, not a unit).
- Defect — Orbs are NOT units. They float around the player as icons but
  cannot be targeted by attacks. Treat orbs as a player-side resource only.
- Watcher — Stances (Calm/Wrath/Divinity) are status effects, not units.

If you see a unit-like visual next to a non-Necrobinder, non-Regent player,
verify carefully before recommending a target.

## When in doubt

If you cannot confidently tell whether a unit is friendly or hostile from
the screenshot:

1. Check for an intent indicator (icon above the unit). Enemy = yes, ally = no.
2. Check the side of the screen. Enemies are on the right.
3. Check the unit's HP bar color/style — friendly summons often share the
   player's UI styling.
4. If still uncertain, **say so in your advice and ask the user to confirm**.
   Do NOT pick a target by guessing.

It is far better to give cautious, accurate advice ("I see a unit next to
you that looks like Osty — playing Scourge+ on the Knowledge Demon on the
right is correct; do not target Osty") than to confidently recommend
attacking your own minion.
